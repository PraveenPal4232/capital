For us, the first quarter of 2023 was our biggest ever in terms of both the number of new relationships, as well as the amount of capital invested. We invested more than $ 50 mn in across 21 businesses through our venture debt fund, and invested in Atomberg through growth equity fund. This takes us to more than $ 700 mn invested in the Indian start-up ecosystem across 150+ businesses. The quality of companies that we have evaluated this quarter has taken a quantum leap forward, as the slowdown in equity flows has motivated founders to re-look at the sustainability profile of their business. We see a sharp increase in focus on margins, cash breakeven and discipline, while the median portfolio annual revenue growth is still a very healthy 60%+. We are grateful that these companies see increased value in our services, and continue to work with us as they scale.

While we invest and manage capital, one of the key ways we differentiate ourselves is by also investing in knowledge and thought leadership. February saw us launch “Venture Debt: The Rising Tide of Credit in the New Economy” in partnership with BCG - a seminal report on the venture debt asset class. We worked with more than 50 founders and investors across sectors and stages, to bring perspectives on how different stakeholders have benefited and plan to continue leveraging venture debt on their own journeys.

Venture Debt Updates

In this quarter alone, we invested venture debt in 21 new-economy businesses, across a variety of consumer brands, B2B enterprise services (including SaaS, IoT, commerce, payments and logistics), fintech and agritech startups.

We had reported growing interest in the clean energy ecosystem last quarter, and are proud to throw a spotlight on one of the most exciting businesses in this space, which is revolutionising the Indian manufacturing capability for high-end batteries that can leverage the domestic mineral supply chain. We began this relationship in the past quarter, and hope to work together with them on an inspiring journey ahead.

Spotlight on Log9

Excited by the differentiated and advanced LTO cell chemistry and battery technology story which could catalyse EV adoption across different vehicle segments, Trifecta Capital partnered with Log9 Materials to cater to multiple capital needs of the company, specific to the EV segment – large capex needs for setting up India’s first indigenous cell manufacturing plant, working capital financing to meet the long cycles of advance payments, inventory planning and receivables endemic to the EV space, and for major R&D expenses to develop new technologies.

Financial Solution Updates

Financial Solutions has on-boarded 13 new clients in the first quarter of 2023, with notable names including Cogoport, Shiprocket, and Beat0.

Early stage companies continued to show importance to prudent treasury management with capital preservation and protection as key priorities for finance teams. The team partnered with several early stage companies like Kombai, Arthvit, and Onsite with their Founders directly showing interest in treasury planning and allocation.

In light of the recent liquidity crisis in a Global start up bank, we continue to reiterate the best practices in treasury management with focus on objective and unbiased advisory, diversification across products and issuers, duration led allocation to match cash flows and regular review and rebalancing to absorb any untoward events and related shocks.
